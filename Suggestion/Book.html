Summary

  Harari describes how the Cognitive, Agricultural and Scientific revoltuion have brought humans to the state they are currently in.

Main Question

   Why did humans become the most dominant type of animal?

Body

   Harrari argues that we owe much of our success to our ability to live in large groups. In that way, the animal 'homo' could exercise strength in numbers. What is crucial to our social capabilities, is that people are able to uphold shared belief systems, such as religion and empire. It is in this sense that Harari suggests modern humans are not that different from their ancestors living 30,000 years ago. The invention of script changed much, as it enabled an accumulation of knowledge, documentation and communication. In other words, it enables our imagined belief systems to increasingly develop.

Final remarks

   It is interesting how Harari makes us realize humans are merely animals. He documents his view on the stages that lead up to the modern history of humans. It is worthwhile to reflect on these stages if we seek to understand the world we live in today.
