Facts

Terror steadily rose from 2010 - Today

Under Chávez, Venezuela increasingly focused on oil export. Domestic agriculture declined and reliance on import grew. High corruption, sanctions and low oil prices ignited the current crisis. Initially people heralded Chávismand its communism.

Rich of oil | USA's enemy

Venezuela reportedly has the highest oil resource in the world (see Link). The evidently corrupt elite favoured a national focus on oil export, which helps generate more money through corruption. An apparent lack of care about proper managament made problems steadily scale over the years. Venezuela has traditionally antagonized the USA and, when problems arose, claimed the world leader caused the country's problems. Hugo Chávez notoriously calls this the USA's 'economic war' against his people. Regardless, they have denied foreign aid, intensifying the existing problems.

Communistic Country | Example Mao Zedong

Glorying communisim, Chávez' implemented a program after the example of Mao Zedong's Great Leap Forward, which caused terror, famine and high casualties with the hope to become a superpower. Like Mao, Chávez ruled his country in a totalitarian way. He censored and manipulated official reports. This control reached onto the economy: where legitimate traders were heavily managed. As a result, civilians resorted to the Black Market and establishments went bankrupt and imports decreased further.
