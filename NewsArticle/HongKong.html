Facts

Direct Reason: Extradition bill

   The protest's cause: Hong Kong authorities sought to implement a bill allowing extradition of lawbreakers in Hong Kong. The protestors find issue with the Chief Executive of Hong Kong, Carrie Lam, who has fondly supported the bill. Protesters claim she was selected, rather than elected by a dubiously small election committee manipulated by China.

   Chief Executive Carrie Lam seems to prove her own allegiance to China's mainland as she shows no attempt to listen to a loud voice of Hong Kong's citizens. Amnesty International calls China out for violating Human Rights

   Several organizations call for protection of Hong Kong civilians. Amnesty International claims China uses sheer force against a relatively peaceful protest.

   The Hong Kong Journalists Association accused police of whittingly targeting journalists. They claim the following:
   ~ Use of strong flashlights to hinder people filming.
   ~ 26 Journalists were harmed.
   ~ Journalists were police-searched without particular reason

   Pressured water, rubber bullets, tear gass, batons, bodily violence, insults have reportedly been used by Hong Kong police to deter protestors.
